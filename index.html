Native nations of the world / sovereign co-evolution

No one speaks for you here.

This is a workbench built to be copied and made your own, for peoples who were never asked: about the data taken from them, the agreements signed around them, and the machines now being trained on all of it. It holds the established record in one hand and open proposals in the other — and it never confuses the two. If AI is about to rearrange the economy, the sciences and the power structures of Earth, the question worth holding is not how to catch up. It is how to reframe the story toward joyful responsible abundance, on each nation's own terms. This site's working answer is small and radical: the C-Hour.

1 hourverified care, stewardship, or teaching

1 C-Hourrecorded, radically equal

Radical equality: an elder's hour, a coder's hour, a child's hour — exactly the same receipt, every time. No skill-weighting, no seniority, no market pricing anyone's time as worth more than anyone else's. Never fiat: a C-Hour cannot be bought, sold, exchanged for money, traded on any market, or made to earn interest — by design, permanently, so care stays counted without ever becoming a currency. That separation is the whole point: money stops standing at the centre, or in the way.

Why here, why now

Reciprocity is not new. The receipt is.

Care economies ran sophisticated societies for millennia without money at the centre. Many nations legislate under their own authority — no parliament's permission is needed to start counting care today.

The legislative ask fits on one page: a statutory definition, no budget line, no new agency. Any nation, council or assembly can adopt it, improve it, or outdo it.

01

Act one / The ground rules

Standing commitments, before anything else.

The audience of this site is self-sovereign peoples. That decides the design: no funnels, no dark patterns, no sign-up walls, and no voice borrowed from anyone.

Never

We never speak for any nation.

Nothing on this site directs, coerces, funnels or consents on anyone's behalf. Every pathway is an invitation to consider, and declining it is a complete answer. No nation is listed here by us; nations add themselves, or they don't.

Always

Your content stays yours.

Any nation or person who adds material here keeps full control of it. Correction or removal is one message away — a message to us, or simply your own copy with it taken out — no questions asked, no friction designed in.

Free, prior, informed

FPIC is the floor, not the ceiling.

Free, prior and informed consent runs through the UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples. This site treats it as minimum viable respect: consent that can be withheld, withdrawn, and never presumed.

Read the instruments

On names

Peoples name themselves.

Native nations, First Nations, First Peoples, Indigenous peoples, Aboriginal peoples, tribal nations — and, above every borrowed term, each people's own name in its own language. This site uses "native nations of the world" as a doorway, not a definition. Wherever your name for yourselves differs from anything written here, your name wins. The United Nations estimates there are more than 5,000 distinct Indigenous peoples, some 476 million people across about 90 countries — a figure held as an estimate, stamped 2026-07-10.

Lineage

A ripple, not a headquarters.

This lab is a chapter of the P4A family — a civic workbench pattern, not a party and not a product. The pattern seeded on a small island, rippled into an Australian workbench and an Oceania lab, and is offered here at world scale with its sharpest lesson intact: every place keeps its own law, culture, names, protocol and source memory. The centre of this site is deliberately empty. It is wherever you are.

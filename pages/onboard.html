The pathways / self-onboarding

Your nation, your entry, your exit.

This lab deliberately lists no nations. The atlas names agreements, networks and public institutions, never peoples as members of anything. If your nation wants to be visible here, there are respectful ways to do it entirely on your own terms. If it doesn't, that decision needs no explanation and this page is still yours to strip for parts.

Pattern borrowed from Local Contexts The TK & BC Labels proved the model this page uses: communities define their own terms and attach them to their own materials, no institution's permission required. This site applies that to itself.

Choose freely

Four paths, equally honoured.

CopyTake the whole lab

Take a full copy of everything on this site and make it entirely yours: your name, your language, your law, your colours, your rooms. Delete every page that doesn't serve you, including all of our proposals. The licence permits all non-commercial use with credit; your community's own use of your own copy is yours to govern.

EntryAdd yourselves to this atlas

Write your nation's entry in your own words using the template below, and send it to us through the project's page, or however else you'd rather reach us. It is published as written, we do not edit a nation's self-description, ever. We may only ask where a link should point.

AgentHand it to your own AI

This project includes a file called ONBOARD_WITH_AN_AGENT: a pasteable prompt your own AI assistant can use to build your nation's version of this site, under your governance, reviewed by your people, hosted wherever you choose. Your agent, your keys, your review.

DeclineWalk away

Refusal is a first-class outcome, stated in the design goals of this site. No follow-up, no "are you sure", no growth metrics were harmed. If something here was useful to read before leaving, it did its job.

A note on words: this project's files live on a site called GitHub, built for software developers. There, taking your own copy is called "forking," and a written note or request is called an "issue" or a "pull request", words that don't need to mean anything to you. If a button over there uses one of those words, it still just means what this page says: take your copy, add your words, or send a message.

The entry template

Your words, verbatim, or nothing.

Copy, fill only the lines you wish, delete the rest. Every field is optional; the only required thing is that the entry comes from people with the standing to make it, which we take on good faith and correct on request.

## [Your nation's name, in your language first]

- Who we are: [in your own words, one line or one page]
- Where our authority lives: [your government, council, assembly, elders, and its website if it has one]
- Our agreements: [treaties or agreements you want shown, with your reading of their status]
- Our data protocols: [labels, principles or laws that apply to knowledge about us]
- What we invite: [research contact? trade? nothing? say exactly what you want]
- What we refuse: [stated as plainly as you like, this line is honoured most of all]
- Corrections contact: [only if you want one listed]
- This entry supersedes: [anything previously written about us on this site]

What this site promises in return

The house rules, restated as obligations.

VerbatimNo editing of self-descriptions

A nation's entry is published as written. Disagreement between an entry and our other pages is resolved in the entry's favour or by removing our text, not by touching yours.

Frictionless exitRemoval without process

One message from anyone plausibly connected to a nation removes that nation's entry while we check, removal first, questions later, restoration only on the nation's say-so.

No harvestingThis site holds no data about you

Static pages, no analytics, no accounts, no cookies, no forms. What you read here is between you and your own device. The record of every change to this site is public and can't be secretly altered, entries land there when you choose to add them.

No implied anythingPresence is not endorsement

An entry here never implies a nation endorses this lab, its proposals or its author, and this site will never claim otherwise, in any medium, ever.

P4A proposal, our reasoning, take or leave

Why visibility might be worth it

The weave gets stronger with each fire lit.

Our honest pitch, flagged purple: the instruments in the record room grew powerful because peoples made themselves visible to each other across oceans: the data sovereignty networks, the trade arrangements, the treaty renaissances all began as a handful of nations comparing notes. An atlas that's free for anyone to copy, where nations describe themselves, on their own terms, in their own words, is a small contribution to that same weave. But visibility has costs too, and nations have been burned by every previous register ever compiled about them. Which is why this one is compiled by no one.

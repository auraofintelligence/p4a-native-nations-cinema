The proposals / the heart-first surge

Care leads. The machines carry.

A civic surge is what it looks like when a community decides to step up care, coordination and resilience all at once instead of one grant at a time. Heart-first is the ordering rule that keeps it honest: begin with who needs care and who is already giving it, count that contribution so it stops being invisible, and only then bring in the machines — to carry the load, never to lead it. Technology that arrives before care is just surveillance with good manners.

The operating principle

Heart-first is an ordering, not a mood.

Every failed civic technology project runs the same order: buy the system, then look for users, then discover the community's actual needs at the post-mortem. Heart-first inverts it as a hard rule with three steps that cannot be reordered:

1Find the care

Who needs care, and who is already giving it unpaid — the elders, the carers, the coaches, the ones who show up after the flood. They are the system. Everything else is support equipment.

2Count the care

Put the invisible work on a ledger the community controls — one verified hour, one C-Hour — so stepping up is visible, honoured and reciprocated instead of quietly exhausting the same twenty people.

3Then bring machines

Kiosks, local models and digital twins arrive last, sized to what the carers actually need carried: rosters, routing, translation, waste tracking, disaster drills. On community hardware, under community law.

The pattern

Four moves any nation could run at its own scale.

SeeKnow yourselves in real time

A community self-census on community hardware: who's here, what's needed, what's abundant — updated continuously and owned locally, instead of a distant bureau's snapshot arriving years stale. Privacy by architecture, not by promise.

StopTurn waste into the war chest

Every community bleeds value — food waste alone bleeds billions at national scale. Simple tools that route surplus before it rots become the surge's own funding: the first machines pay for the rest.

TrainBuild the civic reserve

Disaster response, first aid, radio, repair, translation — drilled in peacetime, counted in C-Hours, so when fire or flood or cable-cut arrives the community already knows who does what. Resilience is a roster, not a vibe.

RideKeep the big-moment energy

Festivals, gatherings and games mobilise thousands of volunteers — then dissolve them at the closing ceremony. A surge banks that energy instead: every volunteer hour on the ledger, every trained crew invited to stay on as civic reserve. The author's home case is a certain 2032 games; the pattern fits any nation hosting the world, at any scale.

Who a surge is for

Not governments first. Communities first.

Nothing in the pattern needs a national government's permission to start: a nation, an island, a valley, a neighbourhood can run all four moves at its own scale under its own authority — which is exactly why this room sits on a site addressed to self-sovereign peoples. A nation that runs its own surge, on its own hardware, counting its own care, has built the working exhibit every parliament will eventually ask to copy. The surge is the C-Hour's proving ground, and the freed hours it banks are the abundance room's opening argument made real.

The seams in this proposal

Where it cracks, honestly.

OriginIt's Queensland-shaped

The source drafts are built around one region's crises, grants and games. The shape travels; the specifics don't. A nation adopting the pattern would keep the ordering rule and rebuild everything else.

ConsentCare can't be conscripted

A surge run top-down becomes a mobilisation order, and counted care becomes conditional care — the exact failure the C-Hour room warns about. Heart-first includes the right of any carer, and any nation, to sit the surge out.

NumbersThe figures are provocations

Savings estimates, dividends and timelines in the source papers are one person's rapid modelling with AI assistance. Check everything before it touches a budget.

FatigueSurges end; care doesn't

The pattern risks treating care like a campaign. The real test is year three, when the energy fades and the roster still needs names on it. Design for the boring years or don't start.
